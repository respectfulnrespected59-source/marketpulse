📖 How to read everything on this screen (glossary)
Signal labels — STRONG BUY → SELL is a composite of RSI + MACD + trend. It's a lean, not a guarantee. Green = bullish tilt, red = bearish.
RSI — momentum 0–100. <30 oversold (stretched down), >70 overbought (stretched up). Mid-range = no extreme.
TTM squeeze (W / 2W) — Bollinger inside Keltner = volatility coiling, a breakout loading. ON = winding up (amber); the arrow is the momentum lean, not a guarantee of direction. Weekly ≈ days, bi-weekly ≈ weeks.
MA 5/14/21 — short moving averages. "stacked ▲" = price > 5 > 14 > 21 (clean uptrend); "stacked ▼" = the opposite; "mixed" = chop.
VWAP — volume-weighted average price for the session; the line buyers & sellers defend. Above = intraday strength, below = weakness.
Options chain — every strike's CALLS (left) & PUTS (right). ATM row (nearest to price) is highlighted; shaded rows are in-the-money.
Bid/Ask $/ct — the real dollars to trade one contract. A contract controls 100 shares, so a "$1.79" premium = $179 to buy. Bid = what you'd sell for, Ask = what you'd pay. (Hover a row to see the per-share quote.)
IV% — implied volatility = how big a move the market is pricing in. High IV = expensive options (favor spreads/selling); low IV = cheap (favor buying).
Greeks — Δ delta: move per $1 of stock (≈ odds of finishing ITM). Γ gamma: how fast delta changes. Θ theta: $ lost per day to time decay. V vega: sensitivity to IV. OI: open contracts = liquidity.
Debit spread — buy one option, sell a further-OTM one to cut cost. Defined risk: max loss = the debit, max gain = the width minus the debit.
Strangle — buy an OTM call AND an OTM put. Wins on a big move either way; loses if it sits. Cost = two premiums, so it needs a real move to pay.
