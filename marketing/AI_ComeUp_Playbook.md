## The 5 Rules (the whole method)

### 1. Small probes, defined risk
Never bet the pot. **Most probes lose — that's expected.** Size every entry so a
total loss on it can't wreck you (the app defaults to a **20%-of-pot** probe
budget). Survival first; a player who's still in the game next quarter wins.

### 2. DCA-tilt in — don't chase green candles
Buy in **pieces over time**, and lean a little heavier when the app's engine reads
the name as *cheap* (oversold / dip), lighter when it's *hot*. You're averaging in,
not timing tops. The DCA Wizard does this math for you and shows plain DCA vs
lump vs signal-tilt so you see the honest trade-offs.

### 3. Escalate winners, cut melters — never revenge-size
Add only to what's **working** (the app's "escalate on confirmation" step). If a
probe craters, it's a small, planned loss — **log it and move on.** Doubling down
to "get it back" is how pots die.

### 4. A basket, not one lottery ticket
Spread across several AI names (the app's **🤖 AI names** preset is your sandbox —
*apply the method to it, it is NOT a buy list*). One blow-up should be a scratch,
not a knockout.

### 5. Receipts + time
Run it over a **3–6 month** horizon. **Track every play — win or lose —** in the
Live Tracker and Pot Tracker. Let the real numbers steer the next move. The
receipts are the proof; they're also the honesty that keeps people trusting you.

## The math of survival (why this works without guarantees)

You do **not** need to be right most of the time. With defined-risk probes:

- Cap each loss at your probe size (say **1R**).
- Let winners run to **2R, 3R, or a runner that 5R+**.

Then even a **35–45% hit-rate** can come out ahead, because the winners are bigger
than the losers. That's **positive expectancy** — the honest engine behind every
disciplined trader. It is a *statistical edge over many plays*, **not** a promise
on any single one. Some months will be red. That's the game.

> **Expectancy, plain:** (win% × avg win) − (loss% × avg loss). Keep avg win >
> avg loss and you can be wrong more than half the time and still grow. Blow your
> risk cap once and the math breaks. That's why Rule 1 is Rule 1.
