## What MAPLE58 is

MAPLE58 is the codename for **Market Pulse**, a free, honest options-research dashboard.
The name is just "Market Pulse" squeezed together — Ma-ple. It is built on one
unbreakable rule: **it will not lie to you about getting rich.** Most options trades
lose money. So the tool is designed around *survival first*, then slow growth.

It does four jobs, in order: **GET · KEEP · GROW · SHARE.**

The core belief behind all of it: **slow and alive beats fast and liquidated.**

---

## The honest premise (say this clearly)

- Most trades lose. Survival is the real skill, not picking winners.
- The tool never tells you what to do. It shows you which way the data *leans* — a
  lean, not an order. The decision is always the user's.
- Every price is shown in **real dollars**, because one options contract controls
  100 shares. A "$1.79" premium is really **$179** to buy one contract. Beginners
  blow up not knowing that.
- It is educational. It is not financial advice. It is not a money printer.

## GET — find a play you can actually afford

The first job is finding an opportunity you can afford, and reading which direction
the market leans.

- **The nudge.** A banner reads the live data and states which way a seasoned trader
  would lean: bullish, bearish, or "a move is coming but the direction isn't clear."
  It always ends the same way: a lean, not a directive — the choice is yours.
- **The TTM squeeze.** When a stock's volatility coils up tight, a breakout is
  loading. The chart shows the price bands squeezing in, then firing. MAPLE58 reads
  this on weekly and bi-weekly timeframes.
- **The expected-move cone.** Shows how far a *normal* week actually reaches (plus or
  minus one standard deviation), so you don't chase a sixty-percent miracle move.
- **The scanner.** One button sweeps a basket of volatile, low-dollar stocks and
  crypto and tells you which names have a setup that fits your pot — and which to skip.

---

## KEEP — be wrong cheaply

The second job, and the one that earns the tool its keep, is losing *small* when you
are wrong — which is most of the time.

- **A probe is a small first bet.** It just asks: am I right about the direction?
- **The beginner trap:** buying the cheapest option on the board, way out of the
  money, where it never moves. A dead lottery ticket.
- **The formula.** As an option goes further out of the money it gets cheaper, but
  its **delta** — how much it actually reacts to the stock — collapses. Below a delta
  of about **0.25**, it's a lottery, not a read. So MAPLE58 finds the cheapest option
  that can *still react*, then checks it against your pot.
- **The 20% rule.** If that probe costs more than 20% of your money, the tool says one
  word: **walk.** If nothing in your pot range qualifies, it tells you the pot size
  you'd actually need — instead of letting you straddle yourself broke.
- Everything is priced in real dollars (e.g. $650 to trade one contract), not the
  per-share quote.

---

## GROW — escalate the winners

The third job is compounding the winners with discipline.

- **The method is three steps: Probe → Read → Escalate.** Place a small probe. Read
  it — is it working or dying? If it's working, escalate by adding a bigger,
  closer-to-the-money position. If it's dying, flip or fold.
- **The tool builds the play:** a **debit spread** when the direction is clear (defined
  risk, defined reward), or a **long strangle** when a big move is coming but the
  direction isn't. Each comes with a payoff diagram and its breakeven points.
- **The Pot Tracker.** Log every probe, win or lose, and watch the pot move — for
  example $300 becoming $333, one disciplined probe at a time.
- **The warning:** the trader who revenge-sizes — doubling down to get even — goes to
  zero. Discipline is the entire come-up.

## SHARE — the method is the gift

The fourth job is the one that matters most: sharing the method so nobody trades blind.

- **Proof Mode** runs the exact signal backward over years of real history and shows
  *every* trade it would have made — the winners AND the losers, net of fees. No
  cherry-picking.
- **The glossary** explains every term on the screen, so nothing is a mystery.
- **It stays free** — for everybody who never had somebody at the table to show them
  how this actually works. That is the *why*.

## How to read a MAPLE58 options page, top to bottom

It is built to read like a story:

1. **The nudge** — which way the data leans, in plain English.
2. **The strategy card(s)** — the actual play (debit spread or strangle) with the math.
3. **The probe plan** — sized to your pot, with a clear "place it" or "walk."
4. **The Read** — a six-step plain-English walkthrough: trend, coil, signal,
   volatility, the play, and the catch — plus the non-negotiable risk rules.
5. **The glossary** — every term defined.
6. **The full chain** — every strike, priced in real per-contract dollars.

## The one-name example (start to finish)

The tool flags a stock. The nudge says bullish. The squeeze is firing that way. You go
to size it: the probe formula picks a strike that's cheap but still reacts, and checks
it against your $300 pot. If it fits, you place it — maybe a $60 call. You read it; it
climbs. So you escalate: two bigger, closer positions on a confirmed direction, capped
so you never risk more than the plan. And you log all of it in the Pot Tracker. Win or
lose, it's defined risk, sized small, and recorded — and the tool held your hand
through every step without ever telling you what to do.
